02
Reverse engineering
Who Google for Business SEA actually is.

Google for Business SEA is the B2B arm of Google's APAC operation, targeting SMEs, startups, and enterprise teams across Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, and Vietnam.

It is not selling search. It is selling transformation. The product surface includes Google Workspace, Gemini, Google Ads, YouTube for Business, and Google Cloud. But the actual offering is the confidence to compete in a digital economy without needing a tech background.

The underlying mission
"Equip every business in Southeast Asia with the digital and AI fluency to compete on its own terms."

The SEA digital economy crossed USD 300 billion in 2025. AI adoption is the next frontier, but 64% of SEA sellers cite cost as a barrier and digital literacy gaps are severe outside major urban corridors. Google's answer is education at scale. Content is the bridge between the tools and the people who need them most.

03
Consumer psychology
Why Indonesian SMEs behave the way they do online.

Understanding the psychological principles at play is what separates content that gets shared from content that gets ignored. These six biases drive every content decision in this brief.

Social proof bias
Indonesian SME owners make decisions based on what people like them are already doing. "Usaha teman saya pakai ini" converts more than any feature list.
Content application: founder stories, real customer results, comment section as social proof engine.
Loss aversion
People feel losses twice as strongly as equivalent gains. "Fee marketplace mencekik margin kamu" activates stronger motivation than "increase your revenue with Google."
Content application: lead with what they are losing, not what they stand to gain. The marketplace fee content is built on this.
Dunning-Kruger in reverse
Most Indonesian SME owners underestimate their own capability with technology. They believe AI is for people smarter or more educated than them. This is the core barrier Google is trying to break.
Content application: normalise not knowing. "Gue juga gak tau awalnya" framing from a credible on-camera host dissolves this faster than any product demo.
Identity-based motivation
People act in ways consistent with how they see themselves or how they want to be seen. "Pemilik usaha yang melek digital" is an identity, not just a behaviour.
Content application: every piece of content should make the viewer feel like a modern, capable business owner. The save and share behaviour follows identity.
Information gap theory
Loewenstein's research shows curiosity is triggered when people become aware of a gap between what they know and what they want to know. This is the hook mechanism.
Content application: every hook creates a knowledge gap. "Udah tau user persona kamu?" creates the gap. The content closes it. The CTA reopens it for part 2.
Parasocial trust
Audiences develop genuine trust with on-camera personalities they watch regularly. This trust transfers to product recommendations at higher rates than advertising.
Content application: the podcast host format works because parasocial trust compounds over time. Consistency of face, tone, and format is the mechanism, not virality.

05
Customer journey
Awareness to advocacy — where content does the work.
01 - Awareness
They don't know yet.
"I keep hearing about AI but it feels complicated and not for me yet."

Content job: make them feel seen, not sold to. Zero product mention.
02 - Consideration
They start asking.
"Which tool? How much? Will it work for my business?"

Content job: answer the real questions they are too embarrassed to Google.
03 - Decision
They hesitate.
Objections: too expensive, too technical, my staff won't use it.

Content job: social proof from people like them. Specific outcomes, real numbers.
04 - Adoption
They try it.
"I signed up but don't know what to do first."

Content job: wins in the first session. Start with just one thing.
05 - Advocacy
They share it.
"This actually changed something for my business."

Content job: give them the language to explain it to others.

Portfolio proposal
Three content ideas, in production.

Each piece maps to a specific avatar and uses a different format. All three are bilingual, native to Instagram Reels, and built around real user problems — not product features.

High effort - Series pilot
Marketmaxxing Part 1: User Persona

"Aku sudah pasang ads jutaan, tapi yang beli sedikit. Boncos nih. Udah tau user persona kamu?"

Target avatar
Budi and Rania. The "I'm spending on ads but not converting" pain. Loss aversion activated by the boncos framing.
Format
Educational Reel with side-by-side copywriting examples. Bayangin client ideal kamu — show right vs wrong messaging. Punchy pacing, 60-75 seconds.
Hook to CTA
"Boncos jutaan iklan? Coba dulu ini." Side by side. "Nah menurutmu yang mana yang bakal disambut? Yang kedua. Gampang kan? Oh... gitu." — "Inget, kalo kamu ngomong ke semua orang itu sama aja kamu ngomong ke ruang kosong." — "Save untuk referensi. Part 2 minggu depan."
Why Google likes this
Series format builds returning audience. Educational core ladders directly to Google Ads and Gemini for ad copy use cases. Information gap theory drives part 2 follows.
